In a quest to immortalize the legendary Kiefer Sutherland in a portrait, Peter McKinnon faces the daunting decision of shooting on digital or film. Seeking counsel from the wise Matt Barnes, he opts for the vintage allure of film, brandishing a new medium format camera for the task. However, a hiccup arises as the test shots turn out disappointingly out of focus, attributed to the chaos of juggling multiple cameras. Simplifying his approach, Peter selects the trusty Mamiya 7 to capture the essence of Sutherland.

As the moment of truth approaches, nerves and anticipation run high as Kiefer Sutherland graces the set with his presence. With a mix of excitement and trepidation, Peter sets out to immortalize the Hollywood icon on film, drawing inspiration from the iconic Jeff Bridges and his Widelux camera. Despite initial jitters, Peter manages to capture the essence of Sutherland, creating a moment that will be etched in photographic history. The thrill of success hits Peter later as he marvels at the sharp, focused portraits that encapsulate this monumental achievement.

In the aftermath of the shoot, Peter is elated as he gazes upon the stunning results, a testament to his dedication and meticulous preparation. The experience of capturing Kiefer Sutherland's essence on film is a triumph that transcends mere photography, marking a milestone in Peter McKinnon's career. This portrait session with a Hollywood legend is not just a job done; it's a moment of artistic fulfillment and a memory that will forever be cherished in the annals of photography.
